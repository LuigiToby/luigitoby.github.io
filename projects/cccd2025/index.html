Overview

The Concurso Colombiano de Cohetería Deportiva (CCCD) 2026 is the continuation of SpaceTech Rocketry's programme at Universidad de los Andes. Building on the lessons of the 2025 campaign — particularly the ejection charge failure that prevented parachute deployment — the 2026 vehicle advances the structural design, recovery system, and avionics stack while maintaining the glass fibre airframe approach established last year.

The full vehicle configuration is shown in Img. 1. Aerodynamic simulation results, including CFD pressure distribution across the nose cone at cruise Mach number, are presented in Img. 2.

Objectives

The primary objectives for CCCD 2026 are:

  • Achieve a target apogee of 3,000 m AGL with a recovery landing within 600 m of the launch site.
  • Demonstrate a reliable dual-event recovery system after the 2025 ejection charge failure.
  • Design fin geometry optimising static margin per Barrowman theory [1] while minimising drag.
  • Integrate a custom flight computer for real-time telemetry and redundant deployment events.

Engineering

Aerodynamics

The aerodynamic design follows the extended Barrowman equations [1] implemented in OpenRocket [5]. A static stability margin of 1.8 calibres was selected as the design target — stable enough for reliable flight without excessive weathercocking in the prevailing winds at the competition site.

CFD validation was conducted on the nose cone and fin-can geometries at Mach 0.35–0.65 using a structured mesh with a y+ target of 1 on the wall. The pressure distribution at Mach 0.55 is shown in Img. 2, confirming attached flow at the leading edge of the ogive and localised separation at the fin-body junction below the design angle of attack.

Structures

The primary airframe is a 98 mm diameter filament-wound glass-fibre tube. Nose cone and fin can are hand-laminated. The fin geometry was analysed per [2] to verify flutter safety at maximum dynamic pressure. Img. 3 shows the fin-can carbon fibre layup detail.

Axial load cases at motor burnout and maximum-q define the structural sizing. A factor of safety of 2.5 is applied to all composite members following NASA SP-8114 guidelines [3].

Recovery

The 2025 campaign suffered a single-point failure in the black-powder ejection charge circuit. For 2026, a redundant dual-ematch recovery system is implemented, with separate charge wells for drogue and main deployment. Deployment is triggered by barometric apogee detection with an accelerometer-based backup, consistent with Nakka's dual-event methodology [4].

Avionics

The flight computer runs a custom firmware stack on an STM32 microcontroller. It logs 10-DoF IMU data, barometric altitude, GPS position, and ematch continuity at 100 Hz. Real-time telemetry is transmitted at 433 MHz to a ground station. All flight data is retained on internal flash and on an SD card.

Results

The static fire test of motor M-001 was completed in March 2026 (Img. 4), validating the propellant grain design and nozzle throat area. Peak thrust was within 4% of the OpenRocket prediction [5]. Full flight data will be added after the competition launch window.
